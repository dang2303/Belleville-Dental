What is crooked tooth? Where does it usually grow?
Adult teeth include 32 teeth divided into 4 groups Minor: Incisors, canines, molars, and premolars. In there, Crooked teeth is a condition in which the canines grow out of the jaw.


Specifically, usually the canines will grow parallel to the straight line standing, however sometimes they tend to grow upwards and protruding anteriorly of the jaw. They will now be called teeth crooked with the position of tooth number 3 on the jaw.
