Wisdom tooth extraction

The condition of wisdom teeth (tooth number 8) often appears in adulthood and brings many annoying problems, affecting health and living. Should the patient have wisdom teeth removed? If so, what do you need to prepare? The following article will summarize and answer all questions related to this issue.

1. What is wisdom tooth extraction ?

Wisdom tooth extraction is a minor surgery performed by a dentist or oral surgeon to remove one or more of a patient's wisdom teeth.

Not all wisdom teeth need to be extracted. Before deciding whether to remove wisdom teeth or not, the doctor will carefully examine and coordinate with many factors.

2. When should wisdom teeth be extracted?

2.1. Wisdom teeth grow in the wrong position causing pain

Wisdom teeth that are slanted or grown horizontally cause pain, swelling, difficulty moving jaw muscles, very uncomfortable. Not to mention wisdom teeth that grow in the wrong position can cause inflammation of the nerve roots, leading to infection. To prevent infection from spreading, you need to remove this wisdom tooth immediately.

2.2. Wisdom teeth grow underground causing gingivitis

The teeth are not fully grown and are stuck in the gums, causing the gums to be sore. While eating, there is food residue that sticks to this position, forming a gingivitis foci with bleeding symptoms, sore gums, etc. teeth as well as causing severe osteonecrosis of the jaw. In this case, you need to treat it immediately!

2.3. Wisdom tooth decay

Wisdom teeth after erupting can still have decay, with the manifestation of the tooth surface forming holes or appearing brown, black spots - the result of the demineralization process of harmful bacteria. The condition of wisdom tooth decay not only causes pain, surrounding gum tissue is inflamed, red and edematous, but also causes bad breath, even spreads to tooth number 7 and vice versa.

2.4. Prophylactic wisdom tooth extraction

Those such as women who intend to become pregnant, sailors preparing to go away... need to have their wisdom teeth extracted in order to avoid complications during an unexpected period.

When encountering the above cases, the doctor will diagnose based on the condition of the teeth, whether their effects on health are serious or not, and then give the indication to remove wisdom teeth at the right time.

3. Wisdom tooth extraction is it dangerous?

Here are common dental problems in young children that parents need to be aware of:

Because wisdom teeth are linked with many nerves, many people fear complications. This is also the reason why many people wonder if wisdom teeth should be extracted.

Accordingly, although located in the same jawbone, has a more complicated position than other teeth, wisdom tooth extraction is just a simple dental procedure. The tooth extraction procedure is quick and not dangerous.

However, if the tooth extraction is performed at an unreliable address, does not guarantee sterility, or has a team of poorly qualified doctors, it can leave many complications such as prolonged bleeding, infection, damage. nerve damage…

4. Procedureminor surgery to remove wisdom teeth

You will go through the standard medical extraction process with the following steps:

Step 1: Take a film, examine and consult with a doctor.

Step 2: Blood test.

Step 3: Carry out tooth extraction.

Step 4: Re-examination after 7-10 days.

5. Notes after wisdom tooth extraction

After tooth extraction, you often encounter the following 4 phenomena:

Slight pain: After the effect of the anesthetic wears off, you may feel some discomfort at the extraction site. Your doctor will prescribe pain relievers to make you more comfortable. Usually, the pain will subside after 48 hours.

Bleeding: After wisdom tooth extraction, Elite Dental always advises patients to bite down on cotton for 30 minutes to help stop bleeding. Within the first 24 hours, you may notice some blood oozing with saliva to form a pink solution. Rest assured that this is completely normal.

Swelling: In some cases where wisdom tooth position is difficult and complicated, after tooth extraction, there may be slight swelling in the cheek area, lasting for 3-5 days. If after 5 days, swelling increases along with pain, please contact Elite Dental immediately to make an appointment for a check-up and follow-up with the Doctor.

Difficulty opening the mouth: After tooth extraction, the swollen corner of the jaw will stimulate the biting muscle to cause muscle contraction. Therefore, for the first few days, you may find it a little difficult to eat, chew, and communicate. After a few days, this will quickly decrease and disappear.
